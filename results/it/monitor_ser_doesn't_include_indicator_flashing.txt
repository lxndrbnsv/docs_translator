<h1> Acer monitor won't turn on - what should I do? </h1>
<p> Problems with displaying images are not always with the computer. Monitor problems are immediately apparent and need to be resolved quickly. This technique often has a status indicator light that can be used to determine which mode the device is in. Although Acer monitors are highly reliable, they are not immune to breakdowns. To start repairs, you need to identify the nature of the problem, which will help you understand what actions need to be taken in the future. </p>
<h2> Major causes of Acer monitor breakdowns </h2>
<p> The absence of an image on the screen can be caused by various reasons. First, let's decide which breakdowns you can handle yourself. The most common Acer monitor won't turn on for the following reasons: </p>
<p> <ul>
  <li> Lack of power - connection to the mains is mandatory for such an electrical appliance. It is important to check that the cable is securely connected to both the outlet and the power connector. The power supply will be indicated by the color indication of the power button. </li>
  <li> Incorrect video cable connection - a special cable connected to the computer is used to transfer the image. You should check the connection connector, the tightness and integrity of the cable itself. </li>
  <li> PC setup - you can install discrete video cards and connect multiple monitors to your computer. In this case, you need to check that the image output is enabled on the Acer. </li>
  <li> Physical failure - there are many failures that will require disassembly of the device. Problems can arise with both the matrix and the hardware. Diagnostics will help to identify the cause of the problem. </li>
</ul>
</p>
<p style = "clear: both"> <img src = "https://en.nodevice.com/content/upload/articles-images/broken-acer-monitor.jpg" /> </p>
<h2> Troubleshooting your Acer monitor </h2>
<p> You need to make sure of the origin of the malfunctions and exclude the possibility of damage to the monitor. Often the computer itself or another connected device can cause such errors. All monitors have a power button that signals the status of the equipment. The light indication depends on the specific monitor model - the blue or green power indicators usually correspond to the standby mode of the device. </p>
<p> The monitor does not have to be turned off after shutdown. In the absence of a signal, such a device automatically goes into standby mode. In this case, the yellow indicator turns on and flashes to indicate that it is ready to work. </p>
<p> For simple diagnostics, you can disconnect the video cable and leave only the power supply. When enabled in this mode, the Acer monitor will display a diagnostic message indicating no signal. If this does not happen, and the yellow indicator flashes regardless of pressing the power button, a professional repair will be required. </p>
<p style = "clear: both"> <img src = "https://en.nodevice.com/content/upload/articles-images/no-signal-monitor.jpg" /> </p>
<h2> Acer Monitor DIY Repair </h2>
<p> Quite often, the problem is the inability to turn on the monitor when the standby indication is working. A steady blinking amber LED and no image output usually indicates a problem with the power supply. In Acer monitors, the problem may be with defective capacitors that need to be replaced. To carry out the repair, you must perform the following actions: </p>
<p> <ul>
  <li> Pull out the plugs located near the monitor mount. </li>
  <li> Unscrew the fixing screws and remove the stand. </li>
  <li> Remove the monitor cover by unhooking the mounts. </li>
  <li> Disassemble the power supply case and take out the board. </li>
  <li> Inspect and replace damaged capacitors. </li>
</ul>
</p>
<p style = "clear: both"> <img src = "https://en.nodevice.com/content/upload/articles-images/acer-monitor-repair.jpg" /> </p>
<p> There are also more complex breakdowns that require careful diagnosis. When examining the monitor board, you should pay attention to the presence of burnout or other defects. In any case, if you suspect a serious breakdown, it would be better to seek professional help. </p>

<h1> Top 10 Common LCD Monitor Problems </h1>
<p style = "clear: both"> <img style = "float: left; margin: 0 10px 5px 0;" src = "https://masterpaiki.ru/wp-content/uploads/2012/10/0-kak-payat-i-zarabatyivat-250x167.jpg" /> Here are the TOP 10 most frequent LCD monitor malfunctions that I experienced on my skin. The fault rating was compiled according to the author's personal opinion, based on the experience of working in the service center. You can take this as a universal repair manual for almost any LCD monitor from Samsung, LG, BENQ, HP, Acer and others. Well, let's go. </p>
<p> I divided the malfunctions of LCD monitors into 10 points, but this does not mean that there are only 10 of them - there are many more, including combined and floating ones. Many LCD monitor breakdowns can be repaired by hand or at home. </p>
<h2> 1st place - the monitor does not turn on </h2>
<p> generally, although the power light may be blinking. In this case, the monitor lights up for a second and goes out, turns on and immediately turns off. At the same time, twitching the cable, dancing with a tambourine and other pranks do not help. Tapping the monitor with a nervous hand usually doesn't work either, so don't even try. The reason for such a malfunction of LCD monitors is most often the failure of the power supply board, if it is built into the monitor. </p>
<p> Recently, monitors with an external power source have become fashionable. This is good because the user can simply change the power supply in the event of a breakdown. If there is no external power source, then you will have to disassemble the monitor and look for a malfunction on the board. Disassembling an LCD monitor in most cases is not difficult, but you need to remember about safety. </p>
<p> Before repairing the poor fellow, let him stand for 10 minutes, disconnected from the network. During this time, the high-voltage capacitor will have time to discharge. ATTENTION! DANGEROUS TO LIFE if the diode bridge and the PWM transistor burn out! In this case, the high-voltage capacitor will not discharge in an acceptable time. </p>
<p> Therefore, EVERYONE before repairing to check the voltage on it! If a dangerous voltage remains, then the capacitor must be manually discharged through an insulated resistor of about 10 kÎ© for 10 seconds. If you suddenly decide to close the terminals with a screwdriver, then keep your eyes away from sparks! </p>
<p style = "clear: both"> <img src = "https://masterpaiki.ru/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/5-blok-pitaniya-monitora-Samsung-SyncMaster-940N-1024x928.jpg "/> </p>
<p> Next, we proceed to inspect the monitor's power supply board and change all burned-out parts - these are usually swollen capacitors, blown fuses, transistors and other elements. You also MUST solder the board or at least examine the soldering under a microscope for microcracks. </p>
<p> From my own experience, I will say - if the monitor is more than 2 years old - then 90% that there will be microcracks in the soldering, especially for LG, BenQ, Acer and Samsung monitors. The cheaper the monitor, the worse it is made at the factory. Up to the point that the active flux is not washed out - which leads to the failure of the monitor after a year or two. Yes, just when the warranty ends. </p>
<h2> 2nd place - the image blinks or goes out </h2>
<p> when the monitor is turned on. This miracle directly indicates to us a malfunction of the power supply. </p>
<p> Of course, the first step is to check the power and signal cables - they must be securely fastened in the connectors. A blinking image on the monitor tells us that the voltage source of the monitor backlight is constantly jumping off the operating mode. </p>
<p> The most common reason for this is swollen electrolytic capacitors, microcracks in the soldering and a faulty TL431 microcircuit. Swollen capacitors most often cost 820 uF 16 V, they can be replaced with a larger capacity and higher voltage, for example, the cheapest and most reliable ones are Rubycon 1000 uF 25 V capacitors and Nippon 1500 uF 16 V capacitors. There is a cheaper Nichicon 1000 uF 25 V (but certainly 105 degrees). Anything else from China won't last long. </p>
<p style = "clear: both"> <img src = "https://masterpaiki.ru/wp-content/uploads/2012/07/20-podozrevaemyie-radioe%60lementyi-monitora-LG-L1530S.jpg" / > </p>
<h2> 3rd place - switches off spontaneously </h2>
<p> after the time has elapsed or does not turn on immediately. In this case, again, three frequent LCD monitors malfunctions in order of frequency of occurrence - swollen electrolytes, microcracks in the board, faulty TL431 microcircuit. </p>
<p> With this fault, a high-frequency squeak of the backlight transformer can also be heard. It usually operates at frequencies between 30 and 150 kHz. If the mode of its operation is disrupted, oscillations can occur in the audible frequency range. </p>
<p style = "clear: both"> <img src = "https://masterpaiki.ru/wp-content/uploads/2012/07/18-vzdulis-kondensatoryi-monitora-LG-L1530S.jpg" /> < / p>
<h2> 4th place - no highlight, </h2>
<p> but the image is viewed under bright light. This immediately tells us about the malfunction of LCD monitors in terms of the backlight. In terms of the frequency of appearance, it could be put in third place, but it is already taken there. </p>
<p> Option two - either the power supply and inverter board burned out, or the backlight lamps are faulty. The latter reason is not common in modern monitors with LED backlighting. If the LEDs are backlit and fail, then only in groups. </p>
<p> In this case, there may be a darkening of the image in places at the edges of the monitor. It is better to start repairs with diagnostics of the power supply and inverter. An inverter is the part of the board that is responsible for generating a high-voltage voltage of about 1000 volts to power the lamps, so in no case should you try to repair the monitor under voltage. You can read about repairing the Samsung monitor power supply on my blog. </p>
<p> Most monitors are similar in design, so there shouldn't be any problems. At one time, monitors simply rained down with a breakdown in contact near the tip of the backlight. This is cured by the most careful disassembly of the die to get to the end of the lamp and solder the high voltage wires. </p>
<p> If the backlight itself burns out, I would suggest replacing it with an LED backlight bar that usually comes with your inverter. If you still have questions, write to me by mail or in the comments. </p>
<h2> <img src = "https://masterpaiki.ru/wp-content/uploads/2012/07/6-zashhelki-monitora-LG-L1530S.jpg" /> </h2>
<h2> 5th place - vertical stripes on the image </h2>
<p> These are the nastiest LCD monitor malfunctions in the life of any computer technician and user, because they tell us that it's time to buy a new LCD monitor. </p>
<p> Why buy new? Because the matrix of your pet is 90% worthless. Vertical stripes appear when the contact of the signal loop with the contacts of the matrix electrodes is broken. </p>
<p> This can only be cured by careful application of anisotropic adhesive tape. Without this anisotropic glue, I had a bad experience repairing a Samsung LCD TV with vertical stripes. You can also read how the Chinese repair such strips on their machines. </p>
<p> An easier way out of this unpleasant situation can be found if your brother-in-law friend has the same monitor lying around, but with faulty electronics. It will not be difficult to blind from two monitors of similar series and the same diagonal. </p>
<p> Sometimes even a power supply unit from a monitor with a larger diagonal can be adapted for a monitor with a smaller diagonal, but such experiments are risky and I do not recommend starting a fire at home. Here in someone else's villa - that's another matter ... </p>
<p style = "clear: both"> <img src = "https://masterpaiki.ru/wp-content/uploads/2012/07/1a-polosyi-na-izobrazhenii-ZHK-televizora-Samsung-LE40A454C1-500x237 .jpg "/> </p>
<h2> 6th place - spots or horizontal stripes </h2>
<p> Their presence means that you or your relatives had a fight with the monitor the day before because of something outrageous. </p>
<p> Unfortunately, household LCD monitors do not provide shockproof coatings and anyone can offend the weak. Yes, any decent poke with a sharp or blunt object into the LCD monitor matrix will make you regret it. </p>
<p> Even if there is a small trace or even one broken pixel, over time, the spot will begin to grow under the influence of temperature and voltage applied to the liquid crystals. Unfortunately, it will not work to recover the dead pixels of the monitor. </p>
<p style = "clear: both"> <img src = "https://masterpaiki.ru/wp-content/uploads/2012/08/2-razbita-matritsa-noutbuka-Asus-X51R-1024x685.jpg" / > </p>
<h2> 7th place - no image, but highlighting </h2>
<p> That is, there is a white or gray screen on the face. First, you should check the cables and try to connect the monitor to a different video source. Also check if the monitor menu appears on the screen. </p>
<p> If everything remains the same, we look closely at the power supply board. In the power supply of the LCD monitor, voltages of 24, 12, 5, 3.3 and 2.5 volts are usually formed. You need to check with a voltmeter if everything is in order. </p>
<p> If everything is in order, then take a close look at the video signal processing board - it is usually smaller than the power supply board. It has a microcontroller and auxiliary elements. It is necessary to check whether food comes to them. With one probe, touch the contact of the common wire (usually along the outline of the board), and with the other, go over the terminals of the microcircuits. Usually food is somewhere in the corner. </p>
<p> If everything is in order in terms of power supply, but there is no oscilloscope, then we check all monitor loops. There should be no carbon deposits or darkening on their contacts. If you find something, clean it with isopropyl alcohol. In extreme cases, you can clean it with a needle or scalpel. Also check the ribbon cable and the board with the monitor control buttons. </p>
<p> If all else fails, then perhaps you are faced with a case of a failed firmware or a failure of the microcontroller. This usually happens from surges in the 220 V network or simply from aging of elements. Usually, in such cases, you have to study special forums, but it's easier to start up for spare parts, especially if you have a familiar karate fighter fighting against unwanted LCD monitors. </p>

<h2> 8th place - does not respond to control buttons </h2>
<p> This case is easily treated - you need to remove the frame or the back cover of the monitor and pull out the board with buttons. Most often, you will see a crack in the board or in the solder. </p>
<p> Sometimes there are faulty buttons or a loop. A crack in the board violates the integrity of the conductors, so they need to be cleaned and soldered, and the board must be glued to strengthen the structure. </p>

<h2> 9th place - reduced monitor brightness </h2>
<p> This is due to aging of the backlight lamps. According to my data, LED backlighting does not suffer from this. Deterioration of the parameters of the inverter is also possible due to aging of the components. </p>

<h2> 10th place - noise, moire and image jitter </h2>
<p> This often happens due to a bad VGA cable without an EMI suppressor - a ferrite ring. If replacing the cable does not help, then power supply interference may have entered the imaging circuits. </p>
<p> Usually they get rid of them schematically using filtering capacities for power supply on the signal board. Try replacing them and write to me about the result. </p>
